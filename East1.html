All about the eastern region of
Saudi Arabia

The Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia, from the coast of the UAE Arabian Gulf through the palm-covered hills of the Al-Ahsa Oasis, to the depths of the Empty Quarter desert. It has historically gained its place as a meeting place for great civilizations such as those of the Levant, Mesopotamia and India, which made it a prosperous trade center.

While Aramco's oil and gas activities constitute a large part of the region's economic activity today, for centuries merchants have used it as a route inland, bringing incense, perfume, and other goods from Yemen toward the great empires.

Enjoy the beauty of nature in the eastern region

Some of the first human settlements in the Gulf region can be found in Al-Ahsa, home to one of the largest oases in the world and the first UNESCO-listed city in the region. Visitors will love Al-Ahsa's natural springs, spread among more than 3 million palm trees, and stunningly preserved archaeological sites that embody the kingdom's culture and traditions. Take some time to enjoy the amazing views of Mount Al Qara, or join a guided tour of the caves and grottoes on the slope.

Discover the culture of the eastern region

For a modern feel, stop by Dammam, the region's harbor, or head to nearby Khobar. Visit the Sultan Bin Abdulaziz Center for Science and Technology (Scitech), where youngsters can take a virtual tour in space, explore the human body, or enjoy watching shows on IMAX 3D screens. Stroll along the Corniche, or enjoy the sea water on Half Moon Beach, for some rest. For those looking for off-road adventures, join a guided tour of the magnificent dunes of the Empty Quarter, the largest sand desert in the world. End your day by watching the sunset from the shimmering hilltops, for an experience unlike any other.
